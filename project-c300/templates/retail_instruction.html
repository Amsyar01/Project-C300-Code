Therapist Manual Retail Vocational Training Intake Assessment

Assessment Venue: Salvation Army IMH
Required tools/materials: OT is required to set up the environment
Tools & Set Up
1. Water pail
2. Cloth (Red and blue)
3. Ladder (inside Salvation Army office)
4. Box of pants; 10 pairs. (Salvation Army office)
5. Box of books (3kg) at Job Club (under radio cabinet)
6. Stationery (Scissors, Sticky tape, Calculator)
7. Simulated rubbish at store back door
8. Broom & dustpan
9. Gift wrapping paper/ stationery
10. Electric raclette grill – OT to retrieve from Salvation Army office and place at electronics section
11. Crib bell – OT to retrieve from Salvation Army office and place at Children’s section, next to half-body mannequin. Client will need to use ladder to get it later.

Therapist Instructions For Assessment

Welcome to retail vocational training assessment.
Please listen the instructions carefully.
Today, you are required to perform the following 2 tasks in this assessment:
1. Display and Merchandising
2. Customer Service
(Orientation) This is the Salvation Army shop. Now, I will show you the setup of Salvation Army, Salvation Army office and job club office environment, including the location of all needed tools and materials and the operations of any needed electrical appliances where necessary.
(Bring client to the back of the store). This is where the cleaning tools are, you can use the basket if you need. You may use the red cloth for wiping. This is the rubbish bin.
(Bring client to changing room) This is the changing room.
(Bring client to the Salvation Army office). This is a box of pants and ladder which you may require later.
(Bring client to the gift wrapping area) This is gift wrapping station. Here is stationery and calculator.
(Bring client to Job Club office) Here is a box of book, later you will need to bring them over to the store’s shelf to display.
(Bring client back to the front of Salvation Army)
Now, can you identify 3 sections and name me 1 item from each of the 3 sections? You can walk around the store now and come back to me once you have identified 3 sections and the items.
***(Note: OT to orientate client to the various sections in the store before starting the tasks).

Task I

Take the books from Job Club and arrange them in a neat and alphabetical order according to the book title in the empty shelf at the books section.
(Alternative: It can be according to height of books if client is not English-educated)
You can place the empty box on top of the shelf beside the changing room.
After that, take the box of pants and organise it neatly in the empty shelf at the pants section.
Please make sure to wipe the shelves thoroughly before placing the books/pants. Please do not place any items on the floor. You may take any equipment you may need
As a retail assistant, you may require to multi-task at times. During the assessment, there may be instances where you would require to stop what you are doing and attend to customers. Throughout this assessment, you are the retail assistant and I may be acting as the customer or the supervisor of the store.
You are required to complete the assigned tasks within 30 mins. If you do not meet the 30mins timing, you can continue to complete the tasks up to 60 mins. Once you have done with all the tasks, please let me know so that I will stop the timer. (Standard time: 45 minutes)
You are required to focus on the tasks at all times and aim to complete it to the best of your abilities.
You are required to return all equipment to their original place after use and discard any waste into a rubbish bin.
You can refer to the instructions list provided to you.
Please do not hesitate to clarify any doubt or ask questions when need arises during the assessment period.
Do you have any questions? If no, you can read the instructions list first and do let me know when you are ready to start.
(Timer) Start the timer once client is ready.

Countdown

00 : 00 : 00 : 00



Minutes

Distractions to be done while client is doing the book/pants tasks)

Distraction #1:
(Therapist to approach client as customer)
“Hello, I am getting a present for my Aunt who is hosting a house warming on Sunday, she would like to grill some meat for her friends. However, she has no equipment. Can you help to recommend and suggest an item and bring it to the front of the store for me please?”
(Regardless what was recommended by client, ultimately, choose the item [electric raclette grill] at the electronic section with price tag and “not for sale” tag labelled)
Distraction #2:
(Therapist to approach client as customer)
“Hello, I am getting presents for a friend who just gave birth to a baby girl. I am going to the baby showers this coming Saturday. Can you help to recommend and suggest an item and bring it to the front of the store for me please?”
(Regardless what was recommended by client, ultimately, choose the item [Crib bell] at the children section with price tag and “not for sale” tag labelled)
Distraction #3:
(Therapist to approach client as supervisor)
“(insert client’s name), can you please clear up the mess over there? (points to the rubbish at store back door) remember to wash your hands after that”.
(Timer) Stop the timer once client has done with arranging books and folding of pants task
(Note: OT to ask client on how they organise the pants upon completion of task)
(Example of prompts: 1. What do you need to do before placing the books and pants? 2. Do you recall where can you get the basket?)
Proceed to task II customer service (role play) once timer is stopped.

Task II: Customer Service (Role Play)

(Involve another staff if they are available; brief them beforehand that they are allowed to give only 20% discount. Let the client know that during this portion of assessment, that staff will be the supervisor, while you are the customer).
Now, we are going to do a short role-play. Imagine you are the retail assistant and I am a customer walking into the shop. Demonstrate how you would serve the customer.
1. Check that client demonstrates appropriate customer service during assessment.
a. Therapist to take note if client greets appropriately.
b. Therapist to ask client for recommendation what birthday present to get for a 16 years old boy who enjoys watching action movies at home.
c. Therapist to note if client is aware to proceed to “CD/Books” section.
d. Therapist to ask for gift wrapping service for this DVD. (DVD is priced at $5)
e. Therapist to “purchase” all 3 items (including items from distraction 1 & 2), get client to calculate total amount.
f. Therapist to ask for 40% discount (get client to ask supervisor for 40% discount but to be rejected; only granted 20%).
g. Therapist changes mind and only purchase 2 out of the 3 items now (1 item must be the DVD), get client to re-calculate total amount again with the 20% discount given.
h. Assume that $50 was passed to client, check with client how much change to give.
i. Now, we have come to the end of the assessment, can you help me to put back all these items.
OT to fill up vocational assessment form.
Feedback to client the assessment result and area of improvement.
Explain the VT consent form if client passes the assessment and get them sign the form via app.
Inform respective VT OT about the new referral
